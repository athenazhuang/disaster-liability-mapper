# disaster-liability-mapper
A tool for analyzing how U.S. disaster law assigns — and fails to assign — legal accountability across the actor chain responsible for disaster harm.
Built as a pre-law research project exploring the gap between catastrophic disaster outcomes and meaningful legal recourse for survivors.
Live demo →
---
What it does
Paste any federal or state statute into the tool, describe up to three disaster scenarios, and the mapper uses Claude (Anthropic's AI) to analyze:
Which actors in the disaster chain — utilities, federal agencies, state regulators, local governments, insurers — face legal liability under that statute
Where the law creates gaps, caps exposure, or grants immunity that leaves survivors without recourse
What cross-case patterns reveal about structural failures in U.S. disaster law
What specific legislative reform would close the identified gaps
The output is a color-coded matrix that makes the liability landscape visible at a glance, alongside a policy memo hook ready to use in legal writing.
---
Background
This project grew out of research on the intersection of climate risk, private capital, and disaster resilience. As climate-driven disasters grow in frequency and severity — Lahaina 2023, the Camp Fire 2018, Hurricane Katrina 2005 — the question of who bears legal responsibility has become both more urgent and more contested.
Current U.S. disaster law was largely written before climate attribution science matured, before utility companies became central actors in wildfire ignition, and before FEMA's limitations were fully exposed. The discretionary function immunity under the Federal Tort Claims Act, the liability caps in state utility codes, and the coordination failures in the Stafford Act framework together create systematic gaps that allow harm without accountability.
This tool is an attempt to make those gaps visible — and to build the analytical foundation for arguing that they should be closed.

How to use
Open `index.html` in any browser — no installation required
Enter your Anthropic API key (free account, ~$0.01 per analysis)
Load a statute preset or paste your own
Edit the disaster cases if needed
Click Analyze liability gaps
Your API key is never stored — it lives only in your browser session.
---
Tech
Single-file HTML/CSS/JS — no frameworks, no build step, no dependencies. Calls the Anthropic Claude API directly from the browser. Deployable anywhere that serves static files.

Project context
This tool is one component of a larger research project on disaster accountability and climate liability. Related work includes empirical analysis of venture capital flows into disaster resilience technology and case studies of post-disaster litigation outcomes.
The legal analysis generated by this tool is AI-assisted and intended for research and educational purposes. It is not legal advice.
